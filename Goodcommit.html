How to write Good Commits

Thinking of a good commits is one of the most tedious task a developer has to do . Sometimes it can feel counterproductive too. But learning to write a good commit goes a long way and keeps one organised as it is better to put 2 extra minutes in today than having to scratch your head 6 months down the line trying to understand what you did.

What is a Commit?

They are the simplest building block of Source Control history. Healthy Commits leads to maintainable codes and the projects become much easier to develop. A Commit constitutes what changes have been done to the code. It should explain any configurations changed; APIs introduced etc. Even if some non-technical person is going to read the commit message, he/she should be able to understand what the code changes are and how can they impact the code. It also helps QA understanding what they will be reviewing.


WHAT TO KEEP IN MIND WHILE WRITING COMMITS:

  • Keep it Short and Focused
  • Describe the What, How and Why. Use screenshots and snippets to demonstrate changes.
  • Add a description than explains the context.
  • Add specific keywords like ADD, TEXT, and STYLE etc. in commits.
  • Commit templates could also be used for guiding one to write good commit messages.
